My unique take on the world.

How joyous this life can be. That probably describes me best. Not that everything has gone perfectly. I didn't expect to go to start out at a small college. I certainly didn't expect to drop out of college and work at a camp. And I really didn't expect to go back to school at a different college. However, after transitioning city to city for the last three years it has been my hearts cry to appreciate the smallest of moments. There truly is so much good in the day to day to love. You have to look at the schedule of today and be grateful for coffee on a cold morning, the sound of rain against the bus, the friends or potential friends in your classes and the convenience of meal plan.

Why It's Great to be a Bulldog

With every year there has been different good things and bad things, but this year has definitely been one of my favorites. In Athens I have been surrounded by such good friends and community. I have gotten involved in a sorority, Alpha Chi Omega, and Wesley which is a christian organization. The classes I have been taking are significantly harder, but they challenge me and I appreciate the challenge. As I am currently requesting SEC championship tickets I can't help but think "it's great to be a Georgia bulldog".
